🎯 Targeting strategy — broad-first

let NewsBreak's algo find the buyers

Every creative below carries its own 🎯 target because each hook speaks to a different mindset. But don't over-narrow — NewsBreak optimizes delivery once it has conversion data, so give it room. The move: set the Conversions objective, pick gender + a wide age band, one interest category at most (Health / Men's Health) or none, and let it learn for 3–5 days / ~30–50 clicks per ad before you judge. NewsBreak's feed already skews male, 45–65+, suburban — the perfect ED demographic — so a broad setup lands on the right people fast. Group your campaigns by these audience clusters:

A · Problem-Aware
Men actively wondering 'why can't I…' — searching for a reason + a fix.
B · Switcher / Compare
On Viagra/generics or shopping brands (Hims, Ro) — sell the upgrade.
C · Partner POV
Wives & partners — and the men they nudge to try it.
D · Stamina / Last-Longer
'Finishing too soon' angle — a different pain than ED.
E · Broad Curiosity
Widest cold reach — the hook does the qualifying; let NB explore.
F · Social Proof
Warm / retargeting — stat-led, for people who've seen us.
Rule of thumb: one cluster = one campaign (or ad set), 2–4 creatives each. Keep the age band wide (e.g. 40–65, not 50–55), don't stack interests, and let the pixel + NB's algorithm narrow to the best consumers on its own. Narrowing early starves the learning phase.
