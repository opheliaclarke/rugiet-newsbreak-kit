What CBO does: you set the $150/day on the campaign only — never per ad set. NewsBreak spreads it across the 6 ad sets and automatically shifts money to whichever ones convert. The ~$25/day* on each card is just the even starting split ($150 ÷ 6) — CBO won't hold it there, it'll quickly funnel spend into the 2-3 sets that work and starve the rest. That's the point: launch all 6 to test, let CBO pick winners. Honest note: $150 across 6 sets is thin for optimising to purchases — that's exactly why you optimise a cheaper mid-funnel event first (see checklist below), so each set gets enough conversions to actually learn.

🎯 How this only spends on real buyers

Two levers do it — not "show to everyone." (1) WHO — set the objective to Conversions (never Traffic/Awareness). NewsBreak's algorithm then only serves people it predicts will convert, inside your targeting — it skips the tyre-kickers. (2) WHEN — day-part to the hours ED actually sells (below). Distinct audiences + broad-ish buckets give the algo room; the pixel keeps it honest.
⚠️ Pre-launch checklist — do these or the $150 is blind:
• Tracking: You're an affiliate, so you can't drop NewsBreak's pixel on Rugiet's checkout. Use a pre-lander you control (fire the NB pixel on it) or an Everflow → NewsBreak S2S postback. Pick the event to optimise: a purchase is ideal but low-volume — at $150/day across 6 sets you likely won't hit enough purchases to train, so optimise a mid-funnel event (quiz-start / qualified lead) first, then move to purchase once volume builds. Confirm a test conversion registers before scaling.
• Geo: Rugiet is compounded telehealth and isn't prescribable in every U.S. state. Targeting bare "USA" burns budget on clicks that can't convert. Confirm the serviceable states with the offer and exclude the rest (Location → Customize).
• Landing page: every ad says "quiz / see the price / prescribed online" — make sure the click lands on a compliant pre-lander/quiz, not a raw offer page. NB's Rx review judges the LP as hard as the ad.

🕐 Schedule — run the evenings, in your own PST

ED is a private, evening purchase — don't pay for the daytime scroll
Keep your account on PST (your timezone) — leave it alone. NewsBreak runs your ad schedule on that one clock. You don't need to convert anything; just use the window below. Why it's an afternoon-to-midnight window and not "7 PM": the East Coast is 3 hours ahead of you, so when it's 3 PM for you it's already 6 PM (dinner/evening) on the East Coast — and most Americans live back there. Your window has to open early enough to catch their evening and stay open through the West Coast's.
🌙 Run: 3 PM to 12 AM (midnight) PST — every day

This single window is "evening" for the whole country in your clock: it's 6 PM–3 AM on the East Coast, 3 PM–midnight on the West. That's when ED sells — private, relevant, guard down. Put the ads ON here and let CBO spend the $150 across it.

🚫 Off: 12 AM – 3 PM PST

Overnight into the workday — nobody's private or thinking about it, and they don't buy ED at their desk. Turn the ads off; every saved dollar rolls into the evening. Weight Fri–Sun — weekends convert hardest.

Net effect: live ~9 hours a day, all inside the national evening, on a Conversions objective — so $150 lands on people in a buying frame of mind, not sprayed across a full day of daytime scrollers. No timezone math on your end — just set 3 PM–12 AM in your PST account.
